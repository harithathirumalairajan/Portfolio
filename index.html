03 Mental Models

My Product Philosophy

The principles that guide every product decision I make.

Work Backwards from the Customer
Start with the problem, not the solution. The best product decisions begin with deep empathy for what users actually need.

Sales reps had access to customer data but conversion wasn't improving. Discovered reps weren't lacking data — they lacked the right insight at the right moment. Shifted from a reporting tool to a real-time recommendation engine.

Disagree and Commit
Strong opinion, openly debated, fully committed once aligned. I'll push back hard — and then execute harder.

Stakeholders wanted to launch AI metadata automation broadly from day one. Pushed back — automating poor-quality metadata at scale would amplify distrust, not solve it. Prioritized governance standards first, then automation.

Find the Signal in the Noise
Not everything measurable should drive decisions. Find the one metric that matters and build the roadmap around it.

Platform had 15 engagement metrics post-launch. Identified that session-to-search conversion was the one metric that predicted long-term retention. Focused the entire next quarter's roadmap on that single number.

Think in Systems, Not Features
Understand upstream and downstream impact before shipping. A feature without context is just noise.

Requested feature was a better search bar. Traced the problem upstream — metadata quality was inconsistent, making any search improvement temporary. Fixed the pipeline first, then rebuilt search on a trusted foundation.

04 Ways of Working

How I Get Things Done

The operating principles I bring to every team and every product.

Move Fast on Reversible Decisions
Speed matters — waiting for perfect data on low-risk decisions is a cost, not a virtue.

Used ROI and change impact scoring to prioritize the GTM roadmap — cut planning cycles by 40% by deprioritizing 3 low-impact features.

Stay Close to the Detail
Never manage from a distance — the best decisions come from ground truth, not dashboards.

Ran Voice of Customer sessions post-launch — uncovered that 35% of drop-offs were driven by a single unclear billing disclosure. Fixed in the next sprint.

Outcomes Over Activity
Roadmaps and launches mean nothing without measurable business impact. I track what moves the needle.

Replaced activity-based sprint reviews with outcome-based KPI dashboards — reduced review prep effort by 50%.

Find the Simplest Solution
Complexity is easy — simplicity takes conviction and craft. I push until it's obvious.

Replaced manual metadata tagging with GenAI automation — 85% reduction in manual effort across millions of assets.

Never Lower the Bar Under Pressure
Timelines change, scope changes — quality standards don't. I hold the line on what matters.

Insisted on A/B testing the digital approval flow before full rollout — the winning variant outperformed control by 22%.

Do More with Less
Constraint is a forcing function for creativity — not an excuse for mediocrity.

Automated data quality and governance end-to-end — 60% operational overhead reduction without adding headcount.
